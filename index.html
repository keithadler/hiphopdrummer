HipHopDrummer
Version 2.0
by Keith Adler

Learn hip hop drum programming by generating and studying authentic patterns — from old school 808s to modern boom bap, G-Funk, and beyond.

Every beat comes with a full breakdown of why the pattern works, so you're not just getting drums — you're learning how the greats programmed theirs.

🎓 Learn By Doing

Every beat is a lesson. Click any grid cell to understand why that hit is at that velocity. Read the About This Beat panel for producer references, technique spotlights, and ear training prompts. The chord sheet teaches you harmony. The flow guide gives you syllable counts per bar based on the actual kick pattern. Generate 10 beats in the same style and hear how the kick patterns, ghost notes, and fills vary — that's how you internalize a style.

The analysis explains things most tutorials skip: why Dilla's hats are always 8ths (never 16ths), why G-Funk uses a 3-level hat dynamic, why Memphis beats are defined by what's absent, why Premier's kicks are almost mechanical while his ghost snares float. This is production knowledge that normally takes years of listening to absorb.

🎛 Full Production Tool

19 hip hop styles with 6 regional variants. 218 curated kick patterns across 18 dedicated libraries. Per-instrument swing where hats, kicks, and ghost snares each swing by different amounts. Named player touch profiles (Premier, Questlove, Dilla, Lil Jon). Modal harmony with Dorian chords for G-Funk and Phrygian movement for dark styles. A bass engine with slides, ghost notes, hammer-ons, call-and-response, and 17 style-specific fills.

Export MIDI for any DAW, MPC patterns for Akai hardware, chord sheet PDFs, DAW-specific setup guides for 9 DAWs, or render straight to WAV. Full song arrangements (2:45–3:30) with verse, chorus, breakdown, fills, transitions, and a dynamic energy arc that builds across the song.

🎤 For Rappers

Hit play and rap over it. The flow guide calculates syllable counts per bar based on the BPM and kick density. It names the exact beat positions where your kicks land — those are your rhythmic anchors. Sparse kick? You have room for complex internal rhyme. Busy kick? Keep it punchy. Export the WAV for a practice beat, demo session, or freestyle cipher.

🎧 For DJs

Scratch-ready arrangements with intros, breakdowns, and outros. The breakdown sections thin out and give you space to work. Export the WAV, load it on your deck, and cut over it. Layer it with your crate finds — the patterns are authentic enough to blend with real records.

🎸 For Musicians

The chord sheet shows piano keyboard diagrams with feel-aware voicings — triads for boom bap, 7th chords for jazz, 9ths for Dilla, min7 for G-Funk. The bass MIDI shows what a session player would play, with correct intervals and style-appropriate articulation. The modal harmony section explains why G-Funk uses Dorian (the raised 6th) and dark styles use Phrygian (the sinister half-step). Play keys over it, add guitar, lay down a horn line.

🥁 What's Under the Hood

40+ years of hip hop production knowledge encoded into every parameter. The drum engine models over 60 behaviors per style: accent curves, ghost clustering, pocket-delayed snares, fill types, bar variations, hat articulation changes, and velocity arcs across 8-bar phrases. The bass engine generates 2-bar motifs, transposes them over chord changes, fills gaps when the kick drops out, and defers to the snare on backbeats.

A dynamic arrangement arc builds energy across the full song — verse 2 is busier than verse 1, the last chorus hits hardest, instrumentals decompress after dense choruses. Player profiles shape the humanization: Questlove's ghost notes cluster around velocity 45-55, Premier's kicks are mechanical, Dilla's everything floats behind the beat.

Regional sub-styles capture the geography: Bronx boom bap (Premier's tight, minimal) sounds different from Queens (Large Pro's jazzy, ride cymbal) sounds different from Long Island (De La's playful, loose). G-Funk splits into Dre's polished control, DJ Quik's raw funk, and Battlecat's heavy bounce.

🎵 Musically Authentic

This isn't a random pattern generator. The chord progressions use real modal harmony — Dorian IV for the warm G-Funk sound, Phrygian bII for Memphis menace, ii-V turnarounds for jazz hop. The swing system applies different amounts per instrument — the way real producers program. The fills match each era's aesthetic: a single snare hit for Memphis minimalism, dense flam rolls for chopped breaks, soft chromatic dissolves for Dilla.

Every style has its own curated kick library, hat approach, ghost density, swing pool, fill type, and humanization profile. When you generate a beat, you're hearing the result of specific production decisions — not generic randomness.
